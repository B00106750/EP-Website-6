Creating a Technological University for Dublin

The idea of introducing Technological Universities came upon in 2011, as part of the Higher Education eco-system in Ireland. Dublin Institute of Technology, Institute of Technology Blanchardstown and Institute of Technology Tallaght responded by creating the Technological University of Dublin Alliance. The objective of that Alliance was to develop a programme of partnership with the objective of seeking the TU designation.

With 20,000 students and being the leader of Technological Education in Dublin for over a century, DIT is one of Ireland's largest and most innovative institutions. It offers career-focused programmes and research opportunities. That includes Apperenticeship, Higher certificicate to PhD in Arts, Tourism, Business & Management, Engineering, Sciences and Health.

IT Blanchardstown, located in North Dublin, is a constantly expanding campus and is one of the key industrial, retail and pharmaceutical hubs in Ireland. Since 1999, ITB is persuing it's mission to make education accessible to a diversity of learners with the focus on a relevant, vibrant, curriculum, innovative teaching and excellent student support.

Located in South Dublin and with a student population of 5,700, ITT offers a wide range of programmes from Higher Certificate to Ordinary Degree, as well as Honours Degree to a Masters Degree and Doctoral level in Science, Computing, Engineering, Business and the Humanities. Delivery modes for courses include full-time, part-time, distance, in-company and work-based learning.

An Taoisech, Leo Varadkar announced the decision to approve the establishment of Technological University Dublin, to be known as TU Dublin. The establishment is confirmed for January of 2019 and the first graduates of TU Dublin will receive awards from the New University in February of 2019.
